I am a visual artist constructing biological allegories. Working across mediums, my art teases out the impacts of the life sciences on the living: human, animal, and other. The interplay between art and biology, theories of evolution, animal consciousness, and the vagaries of biochemistry are themes that have, over the past three decades, become central to my art.

I created my first piece about evolution in 1986. Since then I have made art – humorous, emblematic, elegiac – exploring the following topics: the history of medicine; the physical and metaphysical tools of scientific pedagogy; the hubris of the technological fix; our conflicted relationships to animals; the overwhelmingly massive microscopic biomass; the ephemerality of our present-tense environment; the scourge of the Anthropocene; and always, evolution.

A few years back, my imagination fixed on the topic of deep time. I crave a better understanding of the incomprehensible past. What were the sounds and smells of the Mesozoic? What was it like to rock back and forth in a Cambrian sea with creatures utterly alien? I look at the ground differently as I walk, feeling time receding miles below its surface. I imagine the freeway – constructed of pulverized river beds and seashells – crushed to a leaf’s thickness in a future eons out. I am looking for prolonged chords, where the deep past resonates in the present.
